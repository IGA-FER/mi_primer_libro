# Importance of Regulating Dams

Regulating dams are fundamental infrastructures in water resources management at the basin level. Their primary function is to store water during periods of abundance (heavy rainfall or snowmelt) so that it can be distributed in a controlled manner during times of scarcity or drought.

## Benefits in Basin Management

The proper planning and operation of these dams allow achieving multiple key objectives for sustainable development and safety:

1. **Supply Guarantee:** They ensure a continuous supply of drinking water for urban areas, as well as for agricultural irrigation and industrial use.
2. **Flood Control:** They act as buffering elements during torrential rains, significantly reducing the risk of flooding downstream.
3. **Energy Generation:** Many of these infrastructures integrate hydroelectric power plants, providing a renewable and manageable source of energy.
4. **Maintenance of Ecological Flows:** They allow regulating the released flows to ensure the survival of river ecosystems even during dry seasons.

## The Modeling Challenge

Managing a river basin with multiple dams requires complex analysis. Water release decisions must be based on precise hydrological and hydraulic models, decision support tools, and increasingly, artificial intelligence algorithms. Throughout this book, we will explore the theoretical foundations and tools to understand and optimize these vital systems.
